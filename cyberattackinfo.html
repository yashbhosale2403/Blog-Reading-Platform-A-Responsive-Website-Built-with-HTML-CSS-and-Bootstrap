                Types of Cyber Attacks
Malware Attacks

Malicious software such as viruses, worms, Trojans, and spyware.
Common methods: phishing emails, malicious downloads.
Phishing

Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity.
Example: Fake emails from seemingly legitimate organizations.
Denial of Service (DoS) and Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)

Overwhelming a network or system to disrupt services.
Often achieved using botnets.
Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attacks

Interception of communication between two parties.
Common in unsecured Wi-Fi environments.
SQL Injection

Exploiting vulnerabilities in databases via malicious SQL statements.
Can lead to unauthorized data access.
Zero-Day Exploits

Targeting software vulnerabilities before they are patched.
Often critical and highly damaging.
Ransomware

Encrypting victim data and demanding payment for decryption.
Examples: WannaCry, Locky.
Insider Threats

Threats posed by employees or other internal actors.
Motivations include financial gain or malice.
Password Attacks

Methods: brute force, credential stuffing, or social engineering.
Goal: Unauthorized account access.
IoT Attacks

Exploiting vulnerabilities in connected devices.
Can lead to data breaches or device misuse.
Motivations Behind Cyber Attacks
Financial Gain

Theft of financial data, extortion through ransomware.
Espionage

Spying on competitors or state-sponsored intelligence gathering.
Hacktivism

Cyber attacks with political or social motivations.
Revenge or Malice

Personal grievances leading to sabotage or data theft.
Preventive Measures
Network Security

Firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and endpoint protection.
Regular Updates

Keep software and systems patched.
Strong Authentication

Use multi-factor authentication (MFA).
Employee Training

Educate staff on phishing and social engineering risks.
Data Encryption

Encrypt sensitive data to prevent misuse.
Backup Solutions

Regular backups to mitigate ransomware impacts.
